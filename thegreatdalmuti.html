How to Play

Goal: The Great Dalmuti is a game where your goal is to move up in society. Much like real life, if you start out at the bottom, it's much harder to move up to the top, and if you're at the top, discontents aim to dethrone you. You do this by running out of cards first.

Deck: The game's deck is made of 80 cards. There are twelve 12s, eleven 11s, ten 10s, and so on down to one 1. There are also two cards with the value 13 if they are played individually or are wild if matched with other cards.

Rounds: Each round is started by a lead. When you lead, you may play any number of cards that all have the same value. After the lead plays, you may only play the same number of cards of a lesser value. For example, if the lead plays three 11s, then you may play three 10s or any value less than 11. You may not play two 2s nor three 12s.

Ranks: Ranks determine your play order. The Great Dalmuti plays first, then the Lesser Dalmuti, then the Lesser Peon, and finally the Greater Peon.

Winning: The game is over when you there are no cards left. If you run out of cards first, you will become the Great Dalmuti. Second, you will become the Lesser Dalmuti. Third, the Lesser Peon. Fourth, the Greater Peon. The goal is to move up from where you started or to remain at the top.
